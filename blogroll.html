At the peak of blogging in the 2000s, many blogs featured a “blogroll,” a list of other blogs the creator read and recommended. There aren’t many websites left that call themselves blogs, but here I adopt a functional or technical definition: if it has an RSS or Atom feed, it’s a blog. I regularly read and listen to these newsletters, columns, and podcasts, so this is my blogroll.

(I read using Reeder Classic and listen using Overcast.)

Blogs highlighted share creators with others, either adjacent to them or in other categories. Classification is experimental and subjective.
